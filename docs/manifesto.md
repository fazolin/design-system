# manifesto

Black is the screen before signal. Every work begins there.

White is the trace, the line drawn by data. Cyan is data alive — moving, being read. Red is the only colour for failure. Four colours, no others. Anything else would be decoration, and decoration covers what we want to see.

Mono is the typeface of machines that work. Not machines styled to look futuristic. The typeface is the font of an interface that never asked to be looked at.

The work is about the gap between the body and the data the machine makes from it. The face becomes 468 landmarks. The voice becomes a frequency bin index. The movement becomes a vector. The conversion is lossy. The conversion is the work.

Glitch is what happens when the machine fails to keep up with its own promise. We do not avoid it. We aim at it. RGB-split is not a filter, it is a record of failure between the colour channels.

There is no smoothness. No rounded corners. No soft transitions. Easing curves are the language of products telling you everything is fine. The work tells you the truth instead.

There are no spinners. Loading is a process and it should look like one — bytes, scanlines, letters scrambling. Show the cost.

The camera is not a frame, it is the subject. The audio is not a soundtrack, it is the subject. Every interface inherits the asymmetry of the medium.

The work runs at 30 frames per second minimum. If the machine cannot keep up, the work admits it and falls back. Honesty over fidelity.

Nothing here is for sale. Nothing here is a portfolio. Nothing here is a product.

This is for the person who sits in front of the screen and looks. Everything else is noise.
